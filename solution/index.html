Intro Problem Solution

Solutions:

  • Higher gear suspension, the newer engines for the 737Max were placed above the wing because there was barely any room below. A higher gear suspension allows more space under the wing to fit a bigger engine
  • Landing gear suspension systems fulfill the tasks of absorbing the vertical energy of the touch-down as well as providing passenger and crew comfort with a smooth ground ride before take-off and after landing.
  • 100% takeoff thrust warning master caution sound, the 737 MAX tends to start going unstable while climbing at full thrust which causes the plane to pitch up which can potentially lead to a stall. A master caution thrust warning sound will tell the pilots that they are using too much thrust forcing them to pull the thrust down a bit.
  • Master caution lights are centrally wired and illuminate whenever any of the participating systems or components require attention.
  • Additional MCAS software, The MCAS function becomes active when the AoA exceeds a threshold based on airspeed and altitude. Adding additional software can help MCAS, One solution is software that detects input from the jet's two angle of attack sensors instead of just one can make readings more accurate. Another solution is software that detects that if the AoA is exceeded once. MCAS will only activate once and not repeatedly(considering that pilots were constantly fighting the MCAS since it was repeatedly activated). A third software solution is decreasing the amount of how much the nose pitches down to avoid nosedives.
  • In fluid dynamics, angle of attack is the angle between a reference line on a body and the vector representing the relative motion between the body and the fluid through which it is moving.
